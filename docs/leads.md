# Vylan — Sales Leads (Canadian accounting & bookkeeping firms)

> Generated 2026-06-11 by a multi-agent public-web research sweep.
> **87 firms** across Canada that fit Vylan's ideal customer profile:
> small-to-mid-size (≈1–50 staff) independent accounting / bookkeeping firms
> that collect documents from their clients.

## How this list was built (read me first)

- Sources are **public only**: firm websites, Google Business listings, PagesJaunes /
  Yellow Pages, provincial CPA member directories, chamber / board-of-trade listings.
- **No LinkedIn or Facebook logged-in content was scraped** — that's blocked and against
  their terms. A handful of contact emails surface from data brokers (ZoomInfo/RocketReach);
  those were deliberately **left out** since they aren't openly published by the firm.
- Where a firm's website blocked automated access, the contact field says
  **"web contact form"** — grab the email/phone from their "Contact" / "Nous joindre"
  page before outreach. **Nothing was invented** — every entry traces to a real public listing.
- **Fit score 1–5** (5 = perfect fit). The 5s are tech-forward firms already using cloud
  tools (QuickBooks Online, Xero, Dext/Receipt Bank, Hubdoc) — they feel the
  document-chasing pain most and adopt new tools fastest.

## Suggested outreach playbook

1. **Start with the fit-5 cloud-first firms** — they already advertise "paperless" / cloud
   accounting, so Vylan's pitch ("stop chasing clients for tax docs over email and Dropbox")
   lands without any education.
2. **Lead with Quebec** for French-language outreach — every QC firm here is French-first,
   matching Vylan's default language.
3. **Pitch angle:** replace the email/Dropbox back-and-forth during tax season with one
   secure client-document request flow. Firms already running client portals
   (Fern Taxes, Virtuous Bookkeeping) are competitive-displacement targets.
4. **Before each cold email**, visit the firm's "Contact" / "Nous joindre" page to grab the
   published email for the "web contact form" rows above.
